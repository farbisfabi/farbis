Final Year Thesis Project · 2024–2025

Investigation of Construction of Beam–Column Joints in Residential Buildings with Project Management Implementation

ScopeMultiple Sites, Dhaka
TypeField Research & Data Analysis
StandardBNBC 2020 Compliance
MethodologyStructural Inspection + PM Tools

This research systematically investigated the construction quality of beam–column joints in residential buildings across multiple sites in Dhaka, Bangladesh. Beam–column connections are among the most critical structural elements affecting seismic performance; deviations from code requirements significantly increase vulnerability to progressive collapse and lateral loads.

Site data was collected through direct inspection, photographic documentation, and structural measurements, then analysed against BNBC 2020 provisions for reinforcement detailing, concrete cover, lap splice lengths, and stirrup spacing. Project management implementation was evaluated in parallel, examining how Earned Value Management and scheduling tools were (or were not) applied at each site.
